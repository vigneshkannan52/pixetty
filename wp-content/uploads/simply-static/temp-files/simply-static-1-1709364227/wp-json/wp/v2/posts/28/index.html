Did you know that one of your eyes is more dominant than the other? This article will show how using your dominant eye can help you create better compositions and improve your photography!<\/p>\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n

What is a Dominant Eye?<\/h4>\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n

Humans are blessed with a trait called ocular\u00a0dominance. It means one of our eyes is stronger than the other.\u00a0And that stronger eye provides a slightly greater degree of input to the visual part of your brain. In the same way that we\u2019re right-hand dominant or left-hand dominant, we\u2019re either right-eye dominant or left-eye dominant.<\/p>\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n

With that said, it is possible to have a mixed (alternating) ocular dominance. In that case, your eyes take turns being dominant.<\/p>\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n

How Eye Dominance Works<\/h4>\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n

The part of our brain that processes visual information (the visual cortex) has strips of neurons (nerve cells) called dominant eye columns. These bands of neurons respond preferentially to input from one eye or the\u00a0other. It depends on the information. This is important for our binocular vision.<\/p>\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n

But \u2018plasticity\u2019 in these dominant eye columns suggests this eye dominance can be variable. Some people find that their eye dominance alternates or is challenging to determine.<\/p>\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n

How to Figure Out Your Dominant Eye<\/h4>\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n

When looking at an object, you use both eyes. You use both your dominant eye and your non-dominant eye. Your eyes don\u2019t create an image by mixing together two views of the same object.\u00a0You see an object with your dominant eye, and the other eye acts as support.<\/p>\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n

There are simple ways to figure out your dominant eye. Follow this simple eye test to find out which one of your eyes is dominant.<\/p>\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n

Eye Dominance Test<\/h4>\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n

Remember, eye dominance and handedness (your dominant hand) are not directly related. But they are significantly associated.<\/p>\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n

Follow these steps to determine your dominant eye:<\/p>\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n

  1. Extend your arms out in front of you.<\/li>
  2. Face your palms out.<\/li>
  3. Bring your hands together to form a triangular with your fingers and thumbs.<\/li>
  4. Look through this hole at a fixed object, like a picture on a wall.<\/li>
  5. First, look at the object using both eyes.<\/li>
  6. Then close your left eye. Does the object in the triangle move? If it does, your right eye is dominant.<\/li>
  7. Double-check. Look at the object again with both eyes<\/li>
  8. Close your right eye. Does the object in the triangle move? If it does, your left eye is dominant.<\/li><\/ol>\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n

    If the triangle moves an equal distance with both eyes, you may have mixed eye dominance.<\/p>\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n

    Why Using Your Dominant Eye is Useful<\/h4>\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n

    Knowing and using your dominant eye is beneficial for many activities. It is especially helpful when determining the precise positions of things\u2026 like accurately shooting targets like in archery.<\/p>\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n

    And, of course, this translates into taking photos. Using your dominant eye to look through your camera\u2019s viewfinder allows you to compose better images. Your dominant eye will give you a more precise preview of the shot you are taking.<\/p>\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n

    Benefits of Using Your Non-Dominant Eye<\/h4>\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n

    I tried shooting first with my dominant eye (right) and then with my non-dominant eye (left).\u00a0At first, I found no difference. I shoot with my dominant eye automatically because I\u2019m right-handed. But then I wanted to use my peripheral vision\u2014to keep an eye out while looking through the viewfinder\u00a0with my dominant eye. I found it impossible to do this with my non-dominant eye.<\/p>\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n

    You can try it yourself. Keep both eyes open and alternate between both eyes. You might find that your dominant eye repeats the exact image you see, making it very difficult to see peripherally.<\/p>\r\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
